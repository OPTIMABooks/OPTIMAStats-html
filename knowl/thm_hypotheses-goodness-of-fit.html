Principle 6.2.9. Hypotheses for Goodness-of-Fit Tests.

In a goodness-of-fit hypothesis test, we assume that a multinomial process will have probabilities \(p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_k\text{.}\) The opposite of this claim is that at least one of the probabilities is different from what we specify. Thus, the null and alternative hypotheses are:

\begin{align*} H_0\amp:\ \text{Category probabilities are } p_1, p_2, \ldots, p_k\\ H_A\amp:\ \text{At least one of the category probabilities is different from those above} \end{align*}
