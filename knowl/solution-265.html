We computed then constructed a goodness-of-fit table to compute the test statistic and found the following.

We need the critical value for the \(\alpha=0.10\) significance level in a \(\chi^2\) distribution with \(df = 3 - 1 = 2\) degrees of freedom. Looking this up in the \(\chi^2\)-distribution table, we find \(\chi^2_{0.10} = 4.605\) and sketch the picture below.

Because the test statistic is larger than the critical value (and thus further into the right tail) we must reject the null hypothesis. The three parties do not appear to have equal proportions.
