In this case, the claim is that all three political parties are equally likely. If the probability of one of them is called \(x\text{,}\) then stating they are equal and recognizing that their probabilities have to sum to one gives us the following equation.

\begin{equation*} x + x + x = 1 \end{equation*}

If we solve that for \(x\text{,}\) we find that \(x = \frac{1}{3}\text{.}\) Thus, our null and alternative hypothesis should be:

\begin{align*} H_0\amp:\ p_R = p_D = p_I = \frac{1}{3}\\ H_A\amp:\ \text{The probabilities are not as described above} \end{align*}
