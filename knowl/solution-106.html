Solution 2.4.29.1.

We first use the general addition rule to find \(P(A\cap B)\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} P(A\cup B) \amp = P(A) + P(B) - P(A\cap B)\\ \Rightarrow 0.52 \amp = 0.36 + 0.25 - P(A\cap B)\\ \Rightarrow P(A\cap B) \amp = 0.09. \end{align*}

Next, we check using our test of independence.

\begin{equation*} P(A\cap B) = P(A)P(B) \Leftrightarrow 0.09 = 0.36 \times 0.25\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since the above equation is true, \(A\) and \(B\) are in fact independent events.
