When we had a large sample of 30 or more, we were able to use the Central Limit Theorem to estimate the population mean based on the sample mean \(\overline{x}\) and sample standard deviation \(s\text{.}\) That is, we compute our margin of error, such as \(z_{\alpha/2}\times \frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}\text{,}\) using z-scores with a normal distribution and we freely replace \(\sigma\text{,}\) the population standard deviation, with \(s\text{,}\) the sample standard deviation. Unfortunately, if our sample size is small, we can not make these assumptions.
