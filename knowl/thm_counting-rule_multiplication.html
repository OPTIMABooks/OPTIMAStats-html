Theorem 2.2.28.

The multiplication rule states that if a process includes two steps where step one can happen in \(n_1\) ways and step two can happen in \(n_2\) ways, then the entire process can happen in \(n_1 \times n_2\) ways.

This can be expanded to a process which includes \(k\) stages, the \(i^\text{th}\) of which can happen in \(n_i\) ways, then the total number of ways the process can happen is:

\begin{equation*} n_1 \times n_2 \times \cdots \times n_k\text{.} \end{equation*}
