We first find the critical value for this confidence interval. A 90% confidence interval will have an area of \(0.05\) in each tail. The degrees of freedom is \(df = 14 + 12 - 2 = 24\text{.}\) This gives us a critical value \(t_{0.050} = 1.711\text{.}\)

Our pooled variance is:

\begin{equation*} s^2 = \frac{(14-1)(42.1)^2 + (12 - 1)(39.6)^2}{14 + 12 - 2} \approx 1678.7954\text{.} \end{equation*}

Finally, we put this all together into our confidence interval formula.

\begin{equation*} (742-951) \pm 1.711\sqrt{1678.7954\left(\frac{1}{14} + \frac{1}{12}\right)} = -209 \pm 27.5792\text{.} \end{equation*}

Adding and subtracting gives the following confidence interval.

\begin{equation*} -236.5792 \lt \mu_1 - \mu_2 \lt -181.4208\text{.} \end{equation*}
