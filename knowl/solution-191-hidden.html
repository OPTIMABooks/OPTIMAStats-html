From the t-distribution table with \(df = 6-1 = 5\) and \(0.005\) in the right tail, the critical value is \(t_{0.005} = 4.032\text{.}\) Plugging this into the formula for the confidence interval, we get:

\begin{equation*} 2.4 \pm 4.032\left(\frac{0.35}{\sqrt{6}}\right) = 2.4 \pm 0.5761\text{.} \end{equation*}

\begin{equation*} 1.8239 \lt \mu \lt 2.9761\text{.} \end{equation*}
