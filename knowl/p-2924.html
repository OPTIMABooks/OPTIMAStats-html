Notice that the normal distribution, in black, is the tallest with the shortest tails. The t-distributions look very similar, but they are shorter and have thicker tails. The notation \(df\) stands for degrees of freedom. This is a measure of how big the sample size isâ€”although it is not exactly equal to the sample size. The bigger the degrees of freedom, the more closely the student's t-distribution matches the normal distribution. In fact, when the degrees of freedom is 30 or more, the student's t-distribution will be so close to the normal distribution that we can just use the normal distribution critical values.
