A random variable \(Y\) has a normal distribution with \(E(Y) = 4\) and standard deviation \(2.5\text{.}\) Another random variable \(W\) has \(E(W) = 6\) and standard deviation \(1.5\text{.}\)

Question: Determine if each of the following statements is True or False.

  1. The “peak” of the probability density curve for \(Y\) is at 4.

  2. The “peak” of the probability density curve for \(Y\) is at 2.5.

  3. The probability density curve for \(W\) is more spread out than the one for \(Y\text{.}\)

  4. The probability density curve for \(Y\) is shorter than the one for \(W\text{.}\)

Answer
