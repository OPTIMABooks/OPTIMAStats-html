The margin of error is \(\sigma_x\) times \(z_{\alpha/2}\text{.}\) Based on our work in ExampleÂ 4.1.22, this gives the following values.

  1. For a 95% confidence interval, \(z_{\alpha/2} = \pm 1.96\) so that the margin of error is:

    \begin{equation*} \pm 1.96 \times 4.35 = \pm 8.526\text{.} \end{equation*}

  2. For a 98% confidence interval, \(z_{\alpha/2} = \pm 2.33\) so that the margin of error is:

    \begin{equation*} \pm 2.33 \times 4.35 = \pm 10.1355\text{.} \end{equation*}
