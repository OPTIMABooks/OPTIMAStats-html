The one catch, as mentioned previously, is that we must choose our critical values from the t-distribution with the appropriate number of degrees of freedom. The value of df will vary with the sample size or sizes with which we are working, but the formulas are the same as those found in SectionÂ 4.5 and restated below.

  • Single Sample Mean.

    Degrees of freedom is one less than the sample size: \(df = n - 1\text{.}\)

  • Difference Between Means.

    Degrees of freedom is two less than the sum of the sample sizes: \(df = n_1 + n_2 - 2\text{.}\)
