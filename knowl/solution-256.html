Solution 6.2.10.1.

In this problem statement, we are told what the probabilities of each outcome should be. Stating those as our null hypothesis, we get:

\begin{align*} H_0\amp :\ p_1 = 0.40,\ \ p_2 = 0.25,\ \ p_3 = 0.15,\ \ p_4 = 0.10,\ \ p_5 = 0.10\\ H_A\amp:\ \text{At least one of these probabilities is different} \end{align*}
