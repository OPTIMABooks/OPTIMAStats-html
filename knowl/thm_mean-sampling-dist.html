Theorem 3.5.20. Sampling Distribution for a Mean.

The sampling distribution for a mean of a sample of size \(n\text{,}\) where the central limit theorem applies, is a normal distribution with mean and standard deviation

\begin{equation*} \mu_{\overline{x}} = \mu \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \sigma_{\overline{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \end{equation*}

where \(\mu\) and \(\sigma\) are the mean and standard deviation of the population from which the sample is drawn.
