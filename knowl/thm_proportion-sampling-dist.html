Theorem 3.5.31. Sampling Distribution for a Proportion.

If \(p\) is the proportion of individuals in a population with a given characteristic and a sample of size \(n\) is drawn from that population, then the proportion of individuals in the sample with that characteristic, \(\hat p\text{,}\) will have a normal distribution with the following mean and standard deviation.

\begin{equation*} \mu_{\hat p} = \frac{np}{n} = p \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \sigma_{\hat p} = \frac{\sqrt{npq}}{n} = \sqrt{\frac{pq}{n}} \end{equation*}

This approximation is appropriate as long as both \(n\times p\) and \(n\times q\) are greater than 5.
