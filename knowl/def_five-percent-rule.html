Principle 3.2.26. The 5% Condition.

If in repeating a Bernoulli trial \(n\) times we choose individuals from a population of size \(N\) without replacement, we can assume that the trials are independent and the probability \(p\) does not change as long as \(n\) is less than 5% of \(N\text{.}\) That is, as long as \(\frac{n}{N} \lt 0.05\text{.}\)
