Looking up the probability 0.0250 in the body of negative part of the standard normal distribution table, we find the z-score \(-1.96\text{.}\) Because the standard normal distribution is symmetric, we know that if we looked up the probability \(1-0.0250 = 0.9750\) in the body of the positive part of the table, we would find the positive version of this z-score. Thus the critical values are \(-z_{\alpha/2} = -1.96\) and \(z_{\alpha/2} = 1.96\text{.}\)
