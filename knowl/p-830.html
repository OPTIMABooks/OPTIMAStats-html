Chebyshev's Inequality states that the proportion of values in any distribution that lie within \(k\) standard deviations of the mean is at least \(1-\frac{1}{k^2}\text{.}\) Symbolically, if \(P\) is the proportion of values in the range from \(\mu - k\sigma\) to \(\mu+k\sigma\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} P \geq 1-\frac{1}{k^2}. \end{equation*}
