Principle 5.4.2. Hypotheses for a Difference Between Population Means.

To test a claim comparing two population means, we use one of the following sets of hypotheses, where \(d_0\) is the claimed difference.

  • Left-Tailed.
    \begin{align*} H_0\amp:\ \mu_1 - \mu_2 \geq d_0\\ H_A\amp:\ \mu_1 - \mu_2 \lt d_0 \end{align*}

    (tests \(\mu_1 \lt \mu_2 + d_0\))

  • Two-Tailed.
    \begin{align*} H_0\amp:\ \mu_1 - \mu_2 = d_0\\ H_A\amp:\ \mu_1 - \mu_2 \not= d_0 \end{align*}

    (tests \(\mu_1 \not= \mu_2 + d_0\))

  • Right-Tailed.
    \begin{align*} H_0\amp:\ \mu_1 - \mu_2 \leq d_0\\ H_A\amp:\ \mu_1 - \mu_2 >d_0 \end{align*}

    (tests \(\mu_1 > \mu_2 + d_0\))
